# BONSAIMANCER — Marketing Content Strategy

*Brainstorm session: 2026-04-23*

---

## The Core Hook

Bonsai is inherently a **time + patience** game. The biggest content advantage is **time compression** — showing years of botanical process in 30 seconds. Every video should use the cinematic camera + fast timescale to make something that would take a real bonsai artist a decade look magical in under a minute.

---

## Pillar 1 — "Watch It Grow" (Time-lapses)

The easiest and most shareable content. Record from bare trunk → full canopy across a full season cycle.

**Now:**
- Trunk-to-canopy single species (Ficus, Juniper, Maple) — shows seasonal color shift, bark darkening, leaf flush/fall
- Side-by-side species comparison: Juniper (slow, angular) vs Maple (opposite buds, fast fan) vs Ficus (tropical, no winter)
- Root development time-lapse — nebari spreading outward from the trunk base

**Later (when geometry variation lands):**
- "We improved the trunk" — before/after same tree, same species, showing vertex jitter + junction swell making it feel like real wood instead of a cylinder

**Later (when bud visuals land):**
- Tight close-up of a single branch over spring: dormant → bud swelling → bud break → leaf flush. This alone will get attention from bonsai enthusiasts.

---

## Pillar 2 — "The Craft" (How-to / System Explainers)

Short explainer clips (60–90 seconds) that show a mechanic. Each one doubles as a game tutorial AND a marketing video.

| Video | Systems | Hook |
|---|---|---|
| "How wiring works" | Wire system, wire bite damage | "Leave it too long and it scars the tree permanently" |
| "Approach grafting" | Graft system | "Two branches that touch long enough fuse into one" |
| "Root over rock" | Ishitsuki, training wires | Visual spectacle — roots crawling down stone |
| "The saw matters" | Branch saw, wound healing, callus | "Big cuts need the right tool or the tree suffers" |
| "Soil actually matters" | PotSoil, species preferences | "Wrong soil = slow death. Right soil = explosion of growth" |
| "Air layering" | Air layer system | "How to clone a branch into a new tree" |
| "Repotting mini-game" | Root rake, pot sizing | Shows tactile interaction — most satisfying thing to watch |

**Script structure that works for all of these:**
> *[Problem statement — 5 sec]* "Most bonsai games treat wiring as a toggle. BONSAIMANCER doesn't."
> *[Show the mechanic — 20–30 sec]* cinematic + close-up
> *[Consequence/depth — 10 sec]* "Leave wire on too long and it bites into the cambium. The scar stays forever."
> *[Teaser — 5 sec]* logo, wishlist CTA

---

## Pillar 3 — "Disasters & Recoveries"

Emotionally engaging arc content. These will be the highest-engagement videos.

**Now:**
- Fungal cascade — infect a tree, watch it spread season by season, show the sickly yellow-green tint → deadwood → recovery with fungicide + healthy seasons
- Drought death — stop watering, watch health drain, recover at the last moment
- Over-pruning trauma — heavy cut in wrong season, watch the reserve depletion, slow spring, eventual regrowth

**Later (when gamification lands):**
- Noob vs Expert playthrough of the same species — same starting tree, different care choices, compare results after 5 years

---

## Pillar 4 — "Bonsai IRL" (Educational crossover)

These grow the audience beyond gamers into actual bonsai enthusiasts, who will become the most vocal advocates.

Every system maps to real technique:
- Wiring → copper/aluminum wire training
- Sibling fusion → natural grafting (it actually happens)
- Air layering → marcotting (exact same process)
- Seasonal taper → real growth windows
- Nutrient reserve → fertilizer timing in spring

**Format:** 60-second video that shows the game doing something real bonsai artists do, with a caption like *"This is approach grafting. In real life it takes 2–3 years. In BONSAIMANCER it takes 4 growing seasons."*

This positions the game as genuinely educational, not just aesthetic.

## Pillar 5 — "The Living System" (Dev log / transparency)

Shows the game getting better over time. Builds a community that feels invested.

**Now → future cadence:**

| Video | When | System |
|---|---|---|
| "Procedural bark — 10 patterns, zero textures" | Now | Bark shader |
| "Branches that touch long enough merge" | Now | Sibling branch fusion |
| "Trunk geometry pass" | When vertex jitter/junction swell lands | Geometry variation |
| "Buds are alive now" | When bud visuals ship | Bud system |

Each one is a "we improved X" video that gives existing followers a reason to re-engage and shows new viewers an actively developed game.

## What to Record First

Given current systems, the three highest-ROI recordings:

### 1. Ishitsuki Time-lapse
Rock placement → tree orient → roots draped over stone → seasons pass → nebari gripping the rock. Nothing else in the game looks as dramatic. This is the screenshot that goes on the Steam page.

### 2. Wiring Explainer
Wire a branch, fast-forward the seasons, show wire bite, show the scar. The consequence mechanic is what separates this from every other game in the genre.

### 3. Species Side-by-Side
Juniper vs Japanese Maple, same starting conditions, 10 years fast. Let the visual difference in branching pattern (alternate vs opposite buds) speak for itself.

All three use the cinematic camera, run great at 6× speed-up, and hit different emotional notes: beauty, craft, science.

---

## Platform Notes

- **TikTok / Reels / Shorts:** 30–60 sec, cinematic orbit, no commentary, music only. Time-lapses and disasters.
- **YouTube:** 2–5 min explainers with voiceover. "The Craft" and "Bonsai IRL" pillars.
- **Twitter/X:** Side-by-side before/afters. Single mechanic gifs. GIF of wire bite scar forming is a guaranteed retweet from the bonsai community.
- **Reddit (r/bonsai, r/indiegaming, r/gamedev):** Post the "Bonsai IRL" content to r/bonsai with no sales pitch — just "I made a game that simulates this technique." Let the comments do the work.
